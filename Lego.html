    LEGO CASE STUDY

OVERVIEW

Lego invests millions of dollars each year to hire top global talent. Employee retention at Lego HQ in Denmark is much higher when the employees learn Danish. Lego needed a solution to help their employees master the Danish language as quickly and efficiently as possible.

PROJECT

Danish language learning web app for Lego

GOAL

Help each user learn Danish as quickly and efficiently as possible

TARGET USERS

Lego team members who relocate to Lego HQ in Denmark

ROLE

Lead UX designer, Assistant curriculum designer, Front-end developer

MY ROLE

CLOSE COLLABORATION WITH 5 TEAMS

TIMELINE

10-MONTH SPAN FROM INCEPTION TO LAUNCH

TARGET USERS


We gathered a pool of target users and conducted in-depth interviews, focus groups, and onsite observations with each of them. We drafted personas and analyzed the central goals, frustrations, and needs of the target users. A brief summary of needs is outlined below.

TRAIT:

BUSY PROFESSIONAL

NEED:

MAXIMUM EFFICIENCY

Most of the users had little spare time and craved a solution that would bring maximum return on every minute invested in learning Danish.

TRAIT:

IMMERSED IN DANISH

NEED:

REAL-WORLD SKILLS

The central goal of every single learner was to use Danish in everyday life - not for an exam or a course. They wanted real, applicable skills.

TRAIT:

PROGRESS-DRIVEN

NEED:

INDIVIDUAL FEEDBACK

“How's my Danish?” “How can I improve?” Most were itching for consistent answers to these questions.

TRAIT:

VARIED LEARNING PREFERENCES

NEED:

FLEXIBLE LEARNING OPTIONS

Some preferred to study Danish on their phone at home, some on tablets at home, some on PCs at work.

Prototype 1

lo-fidelity

Goals:

Test the inital curriculum, navigation, and content of a demo lesson

Test Results:

Very positive feedback on curriculum & content, but it was clear the lesson needed more scaffolding and automated feedback

Prototype 2

lo-fidelity

Goals:

Introduce and test a new lesson menu, introduce a dojo-inspired stylistic approach, provide better scaffolding and in-lesson feedback

Test Results:

Positive feedback on new menu and in-lesson feedback; users expressed a desire for higher quantity & variety of assessments

Prototype 3

mid-fidelity

Goals:

Introduce a new layer of personalization: user choice in types of skills and specific vocabulary to learn, and a greater variety of assessment types

Test Results:

The tests highlighted a deeper need for a combination of computer-graded & self-graded assessments as well as a greater emphasis on grammar instruction

Prototype 4

mid-fidelity

Goals:

Introduce new layers of personalization, tested a sensei mascot, added additional assessment types, audio recording features, and additional grammar lessons

Test Results:

Strong positive feedback on computer-graded assessments and personalization; discovered a need for better pronunciation practice & assessments

Prototype 5

hi-fidelity

Goals:

Introduce chatbots, new pronunciation guides, human-graded assessments

Test Results:

Very positive feedback for all new features; the central need was to introduce more content

THE MAGIC FORMULA

To supercharge our users' learning, I relied on two key methods


ELABORATION THEORY

I turned to Reigeluth's Elaboration Theory to design the Danish curriculum because it perfectly matched our learners' need to master complex skills in a short amount of time. It focuses on spiral sequencing of the content and context-rich activities.

2-SIGMA EFFECT

I also drew on Bloom's research on the 2-Sigma Effect, which demonstrates that learners who receive personalized feedback from a one-on-one instructor perform in the 98th percentile of typical classroom learners.



To implement both of these formulas, we recruited two ideal candidates: chatbots and Danish coaches.

CHATBOTS FROM SCRATCH

I began writing and testing algorithms using regular expressions in JavaScript to define the personalities, scope, and conversation patterns of Danish chatbots. The central goal of our chatbots was to let users practice Danish with more of the spontaneity and complexity of real-life interactions.

Risk-Free Practice

Nobody likes sounding stupid in a foreign language in front of other people. You can build your confidence to complete a real-world task in Danish, such as picking up a prescription at the pharmacy, by practicing it as much as you want in your living room with a chatbot until you’re ready to go try it in real life.

HUMAN TOUCH

DANISH EXPERTS FOR EVERY LEARNER

We decided to hire Danish coaches to greatly enhance the learning experience. We designed a tutoring model that was dramatically cheaper than the typical language tutoring pricing model. The Danish tutors offered three key components to our solution:


A go-to expert for Danish questions

Most target users constantly had new Danish questions and hated bugging their neighbors and coworkers to ask for language help. Now they have a safe outlet to ask all the questions they want.

Personalized feedback

Users can submit a recording of any of their practice sessions with a chat bot and get personalized feedback on their performance.

Motivation to keep learning

Many users felt that learning Danish was like going to the gym: they felt a desire and a need to do it, but found it easy to put off. When brainstorming ideas for how to help motivate our learners, we thought of the difference in motivation between receiving push notifications from a mobile app (pretty weak) and having a personal trainer to hold you accountable for reaching your goals (much better). This became a key function of the Danish coaches. (We decided to integrate Drift as we piloted the messaging functionality.)

THE FULL EXPERIENCE

The final product offers a personalized experience that focuses on mastering the real-world tasks that our learners had been eager to learn.

Pronunciation is the single most difficult linguistic hurdle our learners struggled with, so we created pronunciation guides for every single Danish word in the platform. When users click/tap any Danish word the guide appears.
